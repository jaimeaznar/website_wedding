Cáceres & Trujillo

Explore these beautiful medieval towns while you're here for our wedding!

Cáceres

UNESCO World Heritage Medieval City

Did You Know?

Cáceres doubled as King's Landing! Its medieval old town was used as a filming location for House of the Dragon (HBO) and Game of Thrones, chosen because its streets require almost no digital alteration to look medieval. The historic center is one of the best-preserved medieval cities in Europe!

Old Town (Parte Antigua)

  • Plaza Mayor - Next to the old town, with its walls, iconic buildings, and the Arch of the Star.
  • Torre de Bujaco - 12th-century tower with panoramic city views (€2.50)
  • Aljibe & Yusuf Al-Burch - Ancient Arab cistern and tower, fascinating history
  • Calle Pintores - Charming street for shopping and strolling
  • Palacio de los Arenales - Historic noble palace of the Golfín family
  • Calle Pizarro - Lively street with shops, bars and restaurants
  • Convents - Buy handmade sweets made by the cloistered nuns through the revolving windows.

Museums & Culture

  • Museo Helga de Alvear - Contemporary art museum. Free guided tours but reservation required!
  • Gran Teatro de Cáceres - Check listings for theater, concerts, or indie cinema
  • Concatedral de Santa María - Gothic cathedral, wedding ceremony venue (€4)
  • Iglesia de la Preciosa Sangre - Beautiful church near the Cathedral, worth a quick visit

Viewpoints & Scenic Spots

  • Santuario de la Virgen de la Montaña - Best views of the city! Perfect for sunset.
  • Mirador de Galarza - Near the plaza with parking and great views
  • Miradores de Fuente Rocha - Lesser-known spots with beautiful views of old and new town

Parks & Nature

  • Parque del Príncipe - Beautiful park for a relaxing walk
  • Paseo de Cánovas - Tree-lined promenade, perfect for an evening stroll
  • Los Barruecos (Malpartida de Cáceres) - Spectacular natural park (~15 min drive). Unique rock formations and stork colonies
  • Parque Nacional Monfragüe - ~1 hour drive. Vultures, eagles, storks - perfect for hiking and nature lovers

Trujillo

Birthplace of Conquistadors (45 min from Cáceres)

Did You Know?

Trujillo is the birthplace of Francisco Pizarro, the conquistador who led the Spanish conquest of the Inca Empire. The iconic statue on horseback in Plaza Mayor was sculpted by American artist Charles Rumsey. Trujillo was also used in House of the Dragon - its castle and main square appear in key scenes!

Main Attractions

  • Plaza Mayor - Stunning main square with Pizarro's equestrian statue, arcades, and historic palaces
  • Castillo de Trujillo - 10th-century Moorish castle with breathtaking views of Extremadura (€2)
  • Iglesia de Santa María la Mayor - Romanesque church with beautiful tower views
  • Palacio de la Conquista - Built by Pizarro's family, ornate corner balcony

Strolling & Discovery

  • Old Town Streets - Wander cobbled lanes that look unchanged for centuries
  • Medieval Walls - Walk sections of the original defensive walls
  • Historic Convents - Several beautiful convents to discover
  • Sunset from the Castle - Unforgettable views across the Extremadura plains

Plaza Mayor Terraces

Enjoy the view while you dine:

  • Mesón La Troya - Famous for huge portions and local character
  • Restaurante Pizarro - Traditional cuisine with terrace views
  • Bar El 7 - Casual tapas right on the plaza

Must Try!
  • Carne de Retinto - Local beef, grilled or slow-cooked
  • Frite de Cordero - Fried lamb, a regional specialty
  • Perrunillas - Crumbly shortbread cookies
  • Local Wines - Ribera del Guadiana reds

Practical Tips

  • Footwear:   Wear comfortable shoes - cobblestone streets!
  • Parking: Free parking outside old town walls in both cities
  • Reservations: Book Helga de Alvear museum in advance!
  • Weather: June can be hot - bring water and sunscreen
